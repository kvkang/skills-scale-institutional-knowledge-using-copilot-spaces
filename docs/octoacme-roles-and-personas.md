# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and oversee software deployments. They manage release schedules, communicate release status to stakeholders, and collaborate with the delivery team on go/no-go decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release windows and deployment schedules
- Communicate release scope, risks, and status to stakeholders
- Facilitate go/no-go decisions with Developers, QA, and Product Managers
- Maintain rollback and mitigation plans for each release
- Track post-release verification and incident response readiness

### Goals
- Ship features to production reliably and on schedule
- Minimize release-related incidents and unplanned rollbacks
- Ensure all stakeholders and support teams are informed ahead of each release

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with Developers and Project Managers
- Pre-release announcements to support and stakeholder groups
- Post-release status updates and incident summaries when needed

### Cross-functional Interactions
- **Developers**: coordinates deployment readiness and merge freeze windows
- **Project Managers**: aligns release milestones with project timelines
- **Product Managers**: confirms feature scope and acceptance criteria are met before release
- **Support Lead**: notifies support teams ahead of customer-facing releases

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for the end-user experience in product design and development. They conduct user research, lead usability testing, and work closely with Product Managers and Developers to ensure solutions are accessible and user-friendly.

### Responsibilities
- Define user experience requirements and design specifications
- Conduct user research and usability testing to validate assumptions
- Produce wireframes, prototypes, and design assets for implementation
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requirements and acceptance criteria
- Support Developers with design implementation questions and accessibility standards

### Goals
- Ensure product features are intuitive and meet user needs
- Reduce usability defects and support escalations from poor UX
- Increase user satisfaction and adoption through evidence-based design

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and prototype walkthroughs with Product Managers and Developers
- Usability test findings shared with the full delivery team
- Contribution to acceptance criteria with user-facing success conditions

### Cross-functional Interactions
- **Product Managers**: partners on user requirements and success metric definitions
- **Developers**: provides design specs and reviews implementation for fidelity and accessibility
- **Data Analysts**: uses product metrics to identify UX improvement opportunities
- **Technical Writers**: aligns on user-facing language and documentation clarity

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers maintain and improve project and process documentation. They ensure documentation is accurate, consistent, and accessible to all team members, facilitating knowledge sharing and smoother onboarding.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain process documents, runbooks, and onboarding materials
- Collaborate with all roles to capture accurate hand-offs and procedures
- Update documentation in response to retrospective findings and process changes
- Review and edit technical content for clarity, completeness, and consistency
- Curate and organize documentation so it is discoverable by new team members

### Goals
- Keep documentation up-to-date with current processes and decisions
- Reduce onboarding time for new team members through clear reference materials
- Prevent knowledge loss when team members transition off projects

### Typical Communication
- Regular check-ins with Project Managers and Product Managers for process updates
- Review cycles with subject-matter experts before publishing documentation
- Retrospective participation to capture lessons learned and document improvements

### Cross-functional Interactions
- **Project Managers**: documents project plans, decisions, and status updates
- **Developers**: captures technical architecture, runbooks, and coding standards
- **UX Designers**: aligns on user-facing terminology and help content
- **Support Lead**: incorporates support knowledge into customer-facing documentation

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
Support Leads serve as the liaison between the customer support organization and the delivery team. They escalate customer-impacting issues, provide feedback on priorities based on support trends, and ensure support teams are ready for each release.

### Responsibilities
- Triage and escalate customer-reported issues to the appropriate delivery team members
- Provide the Product Manager and Project Manager with feedback on support ticket trends
- Coordinate support team readiness ahead of major feature releases
- Partner with QA and Developers to validate fixes for customer-impacting bugs
- Contribute to release readiness reviews with a customer perspective

### Goals
- Reduce customer-facing incidents through proactive support enablement
- Ensure critical customer issues are escalated and resolved promptly
- Give the delivery team visibility into real-world customer impact

### Typical Communication
- Weekly support trend summaries shared with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Escalation notifications to on-call Developers and Project Managers for critical issues
- Pre-release briefings with the Release Manager and support teams

### Cross-functional Interactions
- **Product Managers**: provides customer issue data to inform backlog prioritization
- **Project Managers**: escalates blockers and customer-impacting issues for resolution
- **Release Manager**: coordinates support team readiness and post-release monitoring
- **Technical Writers**: contributes knowledge base content and support runbook updates

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts define and track key metrics, provide data-driven insights, and validate success measurements. They partner with Product Managers and Project Managers to ensure decisions are grounded in evidence.

### Responsibilities
- Define key performance indicators (KPIs) and success metrics for features and projects
- Build and maintain dashboards and reports to track project and product outcomes
- Provide analysis and insights to support prioritization and planning decisions
- Partner with Product Managers on experiment design and metric validation
- Identify patterns and opportunities for product and process improvement from data

### Goals
- Enable data-informed decision-making across the delivery team
- Ensure success metrics are defined before features ship, not after
- Surface trends and anomalies that may indicate risks or opportunities

### Typical Communication
- Regular metrics reviews with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Ad-hoc analysis reports shared with stakeholders during planning and retrospectives
- Experiment results and post-release impact assessments

### Cross-functional Interactions
- **Product Managers**: collaborates on success metrics, experiments, and roadmap decisions
- **Project Managers**: provides project health data and delivery performance metrics
- **UX Designers**: shares user behavior data to inform design decisions
- **Developers**: partners on instrumentation and data collection requirements
